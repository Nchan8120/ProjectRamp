# ProjectRamp
Skee-ball style Roguelike

# General Gameplay
- Player aims and throws a physics based ball up the ramp towards a series of rings that direct the ball into holes of varying point values. The hardest to reach holes are worth the most points.
- Player gets a limited amount of throws.
- If the player scores below a certain number of points they get a game over.
- If the player scores above the point threshold they get to go a shop.
- In the shop they can purchase powerups.
- Then they progress to the next round where the point requirement goes up.

# Game Over
- Beating a certain number of rounds (10?) will count as a victory on that machine&difficulty
- Every time a run ends the player is rewarded with a number of tickets (more for higher scores, more for higher difficulties)
- These tickets are used to purchase cosmetics to decorate the machines/balls with while you play

# High Scores
- Track single round highest score
- Track furthest round before losing

# Economy
- Leftover balls in a round are converted to money
- Winning a round rewards money
- Holding money grants interest
- Some powerups may give money

# Powerups
- One type of Powerup will enhance you balls (extra money,extra score, extra bouncy, etc.). You will get these from ball booster packs.
- One type of Powerup will affect the play field (Breaking walls, building new walls, bonus for hitting a certain wall, etc.). You will get these from engineering packs.
- One type of Powerup will be more generic and can have a variety of effects. These are called Totems and you can only hold 5 at a type normally, but some effects may allow you to hold more or less.

# Difficulty 
- There will be themed machines each with a unique effect (ex. extra ball, extra totem slot, easier/harder scoring holes)
- Each machine will have individual difficulty settings
- Player must beat easy to unlock medium, then beat medium to unlock hard, etc.
- Difficult names: easy/medium/hard/expert/master/the chosen one
- Each difficulty has the same effect on different machines.
- Medium is round completion bonus money reduced
- Hard is score scales faster
- Expert is minus one ball
- Master is score scales even faster
- The Chosen One is no bonus money
